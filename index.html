Determined, creative, and a natural problem solver.


I'm a recent graduate from General Assembly's Software Engineering Immersive program. Prior to this I've spent most of my adult life working as a hair stylist. I know that the connection between the two may be hard to see at first, but they have more in common than you might think.

I consider myself to have specialized in hair coloring, which is much more of a science than an art. Mastery of hair color requires a thorough knowledge of the product being used, a strong understanding of the medium with which you're working, and a firm grasp on hair color theory. Additionally, you must be able to communicate effectively with the client to determine exactly what their goals are and the steps you will be taking to reach them. It's the responsibility of a skilled colorist to take all that information and return a result that meets the goals and expectations set. It's seldom a straight-forward solution.

I've been able to extract from my years as a hair stylist some valuable tools and apply them to my true passion: coding. Problem solving, adaptability, critical thinking, collaboration, communcation, and personal responsibility. I'm able to take these skills and use them to create custom applications either from my own imagining or by request. I especially love creating logic that provides an enjoyable experience for the user as well as putting together an aesthetically pleasing product.

I'm so excited to have finally taken this step and can't wait to see what I am able to contribute to the community!

Portfolio

This is a game I created for my final project for General Assembly's Software Engineering Immersive program. This was an opportunity for me to work with the Phaser 3 framework as well as get some experience with game development. This game performs all CRUD actions aside from auth. You can create, load and delete characters, and when a shell is picked up, the inventory is updated in the database, as well as when you open your inventory in game and click on a shell. Express, MongoDB, and Mongoose were used for the back end.



I worked in part of a four person team to create this e-commerce website. We used ReactJS and implemented Stripe for payments. This was a great experience working with a group and utilizing version control with GitHub.



This was my first full stack project that I worked on independently. It's an ode to retro text-based games. The object was to create a project that performed all CRUD actions aside from auth. Users can create games, view past ones, and delete the records of the past games. Similar to my other game, I used the inventory as a way to do patch requests, by adding and removing items from your inventory as you go through the game. This was made using only HTML, CSS, and JavaScript for the front end and Express, MongoDB, and Mongoose for the back.
